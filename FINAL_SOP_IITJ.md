**Statement of Purpose**
PhD Application – School of Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, IIT Jodhpur

My interest in artificial intelligence, particularly in medical imaging, is rooted in a fundamental concern: modern AI systems often lack the transparency required for real-world clinical adoption. While contemporary deep learning models demonstrate strong performance on benchmark datasets, their opaque decision-making processes limit their utility in healthcare, where interpretability is not optional but essential. This realization, reinforced through both my academic training and independent work, has shaped my decision to pursue doctoral research in explainable AI (XAI) for medical imaging.

I am currently pursuing my M.Tech at IIT Jodhpur in the School of Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, where I have developed a focused interest in multimodal deep learning and explainability. My academic journey has been complemented by three years of prior professional experience as a full-stack developer. While this experience strengthened my engineering discipline and system-level thinking, I found that purely product-driven environments did not align with my long-term goal of contributing to foundational advancements in AI. This shift in perspective motivated my transition toward research-oriented work during my postgraduate studies.

My master’s thesis, titled *“Unified Multi-Domain Diagnostic Pipeline with Multimodal Explainability,”* represents the core of my research preparation. In this work, I designed and implemented an end-to-end multimodal deep learning framework that integrates heterogeneous medical datasets across radiology, dermatology, ophthalmology, and neurology. The system leverages a Swin Transformer V2 backbone for visual feature extraction and incorporates structured Electronic Health Record (EHR) data through a custom fusion architecture. Additionally, I integrated vision-language models to generate clinically meaningful diagnostic narratives, thereby augmenting traditional classification outputs with interpretable textual reasoning.

A key contribution of this work lies in addressing the fragmentation of medical datasets, enabling unified learning across 52 concurrent disease targets. Beyond performance metrics such as stable convergence and strong AUC-ROC scores, the system emphasizes explainability by producing both visual and textual justifications for predictions. I further extended this research into a prototype deployment pipeline using FastAPI and a React Native interface, demonstrating the feasibility of edge-level clinical integration. This project reflects my ability to independently conceptualize and execute complex research systems, combining deep learning, data engineering, and applied deployment.

My primary research interest for doctoral study is the development of robust, explainable, and clinically deployable AI systems for medical imaging. I am particularly interested in exploring how multimodal architectures and vision-language models can be designed to provide inherently interpretable outputs, rather than relying on post-hoc explanation techniques. I aim to work on applied and experimental research that bridges the gap between theoretical model design and real-world clinical usability, with a specific focus on trust, reliability, and scalability.

Pursuing a PhD at IIT Jodhpur is a natural continuation of my academic journey. My experience during the M.Tech program has provided me with a strong foundational understanding of the institute’s academic environment, and I am highly motivated to further engage with its research ecosystem. The interdisciplinary nature of the School of Artificial Intelligence and Data Science, combined with access to computational resources and collaborative opportunities, provides an ideal platform for advancing my research goals. Continuing at the same institution also allows me to build upon my existing work with continuity and depth.

While my academic record includes certain limitations, such as modest performance in earlier stages of my education and the absence of formal publications at this point, I have actively addressed these gaps through sustained independent research and practical implementation. My master’s thesis, in particular, demonstrates my ability to operate at the level of complexity expected in rigorous research environments. I view doctoral study as the necessary opportunity to strengthen my theoretical grounding, contribute original research, and formalize my systems-level work through peer-reviewed publications.

In the long term, I aim to work as a research scientist in industry or applied research labs, focusing on the development of trustworthy AI systems for healthcare. My goal is to contribute to bridging the gap between experimental machine learning models and clinically approved diagnostic tools. A PhD will equip me with the critical rigor, research exposure, and interdisciplinary collaboration required to achieve this objective.

In summary, my academic training, professional software engineering experience, and focused research efforts have prepared me to pursue doctoral research in explainable medical AI. I am committed to contributing meaningful advancements to this domain, with an emphasis on building systems that are not only accurate but also transparent, ethical, and reliable in real-world clinical settings.
